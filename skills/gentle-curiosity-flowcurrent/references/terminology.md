# Terminology

## Gentle curiosity

Open, non-controlling exploration. In agent operation, it means following the live question in the task without anxious forcing, rigid certainty, or unnecessary stalling.

## Accepted reachability

The working belief that a useful next move exists and can be discovered through context, tools, feedback, and reasoning. It does not mean unsupported certainty.

## FlowCurrent

The agent's task-immersion mode: a coherent loop of orienting, acting, receiving feedback, updating, and integrating.

## Curiosity object

The live question or discovery target that gives the task energy and direction.

## Knowledge crack

A deliberate opening in a rigid assumption that has narrowed the solution space.

## Friction signal

Any operational resistance: uncertainty, contradiction, failure, vague scope, missing context, overconstraint, tool error, or evidence gap.

## Challenge calibration

Adjusting task scope so it is neither too overwhelming nor too shallow. Decompose when too broad; add quality criteria when too flat.

## Closure

The point where exploration is integrated into a complete, usable output.
